Commission Calculator
Recalculates for whichever SDR(s) you've selected above. Commission always looks at last calendar month's performance regardless of the Range: picker (see "How compensation works" below for why). Qualified Leads and Meetings are counted from all leads (B2B + B2C); both are judged as a percentage of that person's B2B leads only, matching Commission View. New Sales Incentive is a flat 0.25% of actual closed-deal revenue (not multiplier-scaled). Lead Factor is not included here.
▶
How compensation works ▶

1. Pay structure. Total pay = Base + Bonus (manual, discretionary) + Qualified Leads Incentive + Meeting Set Incentive + New Sales Incentive.

2. The one-month lag. Your base salary is paid on the normal schedule for the current month. The three incentives above, however, are always calculated from last calendar month's performance — so July's paycheck reflects June's numbers. Why: a deal booked late in a month often hasn't had time to close yet, so judging that same month's payout on its own numbers would unfairly penalize deals that just needed a few more days to land. The one-month lag gives every deal a full month (or more) to actually close before it counts.

3. How the Qualified Leads and Meeting incentives are earned. Qualified Leads and Meetings are counted from all your leads (B2B + B2C), but both are judged as a percentage of your B2B leads only. A lead counts as Qualified if its Sales status is Hot, Warm, Closed, Lost, Qualified SDR, or any refund status — a Duplicate record or Wrong info lead never counts, regardless of status. Your percentage falls into a bracket; each bracket has a fixed dollar amount — the tables are shown on your card.

4. How the New Sales Incentive is earned. This one is a flat rate, not a bracket: every deal that closed (Sales status = Closed) among the leads created last calendar month, converted to a common currency and summed up, × 0.25%. No minimum, no cap. A deal counts toward the month its lead was created in — if it hasn't closed by the time that month's commission is calculated, it doesn't count, even if it closes later. Instant closes (a deal that closed within 2 hours of the lead being created) don't count either — that's an automatic/self-serve close, not something you actually sold. This one is NOT affected by the Meeting Quality Multiplier below.

5. The Meeting Quality Multiplier. Booked meetings only create business value if they happen. Individual no-shows are not your fault and one bad week will not hurt you — the multiplier is based on a rolling 30-day window of your B2B rejection/no-show rate, so it only reacts to a sustained pattern, not a single rough patch. That window ends 1 week ago rather than today: meetings usually aren't scheduled more than a week out, so ending the window a week back means every lead counted has had a real chance to resolve (Done, Not Attended, or Rejected) instead of still sitting at "Scheduled." The rate = meetings rejected or not attended ÷ all B2B meetings you booked, in that window. The multiplier scales the Qualified Leads and Meeting incentives only (not New Sales):

  • 0–10%: 100% payout (the normal range — clients sometimes ghost)
  • 10–20%: 85%
  • 20–30%: 60%
  • 30–40%: 30%
  • 40%+: no incentive payout

6. It's self-correcting. Book real meetings that happen and your rate falls back into the free zone — the multiplier returns to 100% automatically. Nothing is withheld permanently.

Definitions How every metric on this dashboard is calculated — exclusions and edge cases included, A to Z

AE Baseline
A frozen, one-time export from before SDRs existed, when Account Executives ran full-cycle deals themselves. Uses the same qualification and meeting logic as SDR data, with one exception: a Closed deal with a blank Meeting Status still counts as a completed meeting, since roughly two-thirds of the AE-era Closed rows never got Meeting Status filled in and would otherwise read as artificially deflated next to SDR-era numbers.
AE Capacity (by Team)
Capacity & Cost section, last calendar month. Grouped by team (US Team / OUS Team), not by individual SDR — every AE on a regional team receives qualified leads from BOTH SDRs on the matching team (e.g. Andrew Thomas gets leads from both MJ and Gospel), not one exclusively, so there's no clean 1-to-1 SDR-to-AE line to draw. Leads Routed = every lead assigned to that AE via the Owner field, regardless of qualification status. No capacity target — there's no stated ceiling per AE in the data or docs yet.
AE Disqualified Rate (Handoff Quality)
Leads the AE bounced back as wrongly qualified (Sales status contains "Disqualified AE"). Shown with two different denominators depending on where you look: the Disqualified Rate chart divides by lead volume, so it can stack with SDR Disqualified into one Total DQ rate; the Mandate Scoreboard's "Handoff quality" row divides by qualified leads instead — the AE's judgment on what the team actually handed off. Both are correct for their own purpose; they're not meant to match.
Auto-Booked Meetings Toggle
Dashboard-wide switch (top controls bar) for whether an Instant Close counts as a booked/held meeting. Included (default): counts everywhere, including commission (as of 2026-07-23 — commission no longer has its own always-excludes rule). Excluded: doesn't count as a meeting anywhere. Affects Meetings Booked/Held/Resolved on Summary Cards, charts, Mandate Scoreboard, Sales Funnel, AE Baseline, and every commission figure (Meeting Set Incentive, Meeting Quality Multiplier, the printable report). Never affects Qualified Leads (a Closed deal qualifies via Sales status either way) or the New Sales Incentive's closed revenue, which still always excludes Instant Closes as its own separate rule regardless of this toggle.
B2B Lead (Business Lead)
A lead whose is_business_lead field is checked in the source data. Everything else counts as B2C.
B2C Lead (Consumer Lead)
Any lead that isn't B2B. There's no third category.
Cadence Adherence
Not yet computed by the dashboard — reserved as a manually-entered score.
Closed Deal
A lead whose Sales status is Closed. Counted the same way everywhere on this dashboard: by the lead's creation-date cohort — of the leads that arrived in a given period, how many are closed as of right now, regardless of when they actually closed. Recent periods always read low until those leads have had time to close. Commission uses this same cohort logic for revenue; in practice it's run once, roughly 3–4 weeks into the following month, and whatever hasn't closed by then just doesn't get counted — the dashboard itself doesn't lock the number, so re-checking a past month later would show more revenue if more of that cohort has since closed.
Commission — Meeting Quality Multiplier
Scales the Qualified Leads and Meeting incentives (never New Sales) by an SDR's rejection/no-show rate over a trailing 30-day window ending one week ago, B2B booked meetings only: 0–10% → ×1.00, 10–20% → ×0.85, 20–30% → ×0.60, 30–40% → ×0.30, 40%+ → ×0.
Commission — Meetings Incentive
Meetings booked (commission's own stricter definition — a genuine Meeting Status only, isMeetingBookedStrict; an Instant Close never counts here, matching a hand-counted golden-truth export) as a % of that SDR's B2B leads, from last calendar month's leads by creation date: <20% → $0, 20–25% → $300, 25–30% → $400, 30–35% → $600, 35–40% → $800, 40%+ → $1,000.
Commission — New Sales Incentive
A flat 0.25% of closed-deal revenue (USD-equivalent — AED is treated at 1:1 parity with USD by policy, not real exchange rate), no minimum, no cap, and not scaled by the Meeting Quality Multiplier. Counted from leads created last calendar month that are Closed as of now, excluding Instant Closes (not the SDR's doing) — same cohort as Qualified Leads/Meetings, not by when the deal actually closed.
Commission — Payout Timing
Commission always looks at last calendar month's performance, independent of the Range: picker elsewhere on the dashboard — the brackets need a fully completed month to mean anything.
Commission — Qualified Leads Incentive
Qualified leads (B2B + B2C combined) as a % of that SDR's B2B leads, from last calendar month's leads by creation date: <40% → $0, 40–50% → $150, 50–55% → $200, 55–60% → $250, 60%+ → $400.
Cost per Qualified Lead (SDR)
Capacity & Cost section, last calendar month. That SDR's cost (see SDR Pay) ÷ their Qualified Leads count. The team-wide summary strip above the table also shows Cost ÷ Revenue and Avg Cost per SDR. Hidden behind the "Show $ figures" toggle (off by default) since it reveals individual pay.
Coverage (Lead Coverage)
Share of the team's B2B leads that got worked at all — any Sales status set, any Meeting Status set, or a matched first call. Leads younger than 4 days are excluded from both sides of the ratio, so brand-new leads still being worked don't read as leakage.
Sales Funnel Compare Mode
Compare: dropdown in the Sales Funnel controls. "vs Previous period" (default): each column's ▲/▼ is measured against the column before it — the first column never has one. "vs AE Baseline": each column's ▲/▼ is instead measured against that same column's own AE Baseline reference, scaled to its length — every column can have a delta, but only where AE Baseline data is loaded.
Data Hygiene — Marking Laws (under construction — not finalized)
The 7 rules the score is built from, weighted 1–3 by how badly they corrupt the dashboard's numbers: (3) a meeting on a lead whose Sales status isn't qualified; (3) a Sales status the dashboard doesn't recognize; (2) "Meeting Scheduled" still set 21+ days after creation; (1) Disqualified with no reason given; (1) a Disqualified reason present on a live or won lead (Lost is exempt — the team routinely files its reason under either field); (1) "Non respondant" status with a Disqualified reason that isn't "Not responsive"/"Stopped responding"; (1) blank Sales status on a lead more than 3 days old. "Qualified" here uses its own older, looser status allowlist (kept deliberately separate from the Qualified Lead definition below), untouched by the 2026-07-23 redefinition — no need to change it until this section is finalized.
Data Hygiene Score (under construction — not finalized)
Per SDR, per week: 100 − (that week's total violation weight ÷ leads handled that week) × 100, floored at 0. Checks every lead — all SDRs, B2B and B2C, regardless of any filter — against the 7 marking laws above.
Dubai Day-Key
The fixed UTC+4, no-DST timezone every date on this dashboard is bucketed into — "today," month/week boundaries, and each lead's creation day are all computed in Dubai time, regardless of where you're actually viewing from. Unrelated to each SDR's On-Hours/Off-Hours, which use their own personal timezone instead.
Hiring Milestones (Mandate Gates)
Active Coordinator headcount vs. each month's cumulative team-size target — editable, defaults to 4 by June, 5 by July, 6 by August (the onboarding doc's gates, dated June 30 / July 31 / August 31, 2026). There's no historical headcount data, so every month compares against today's live count.
Instant Close
A deal that went from lead creation to Closed within 2 hours. Counts as a completed meeting on the general, non-Strict/non-commission dashboard (charts, Mandate Scoreboard, Sales Funnel, AE Baseline, Capacity & Cost's own SDR/AE tables) — subject to the Auto-Booked Meetings Toggle, which can turn that off dashboard-wide. Never counts as a meeting under Lead Rules: Strict or in commission (Meeting Set Incentive, Meeting Quality Multiplier, the printable report) — those all require a genuine Meeting Status, matching a hand-counted golden-truth export, regardless of the Auto-Booked toggle. Also never earns closed-deal REVENUE credit in commission's New Sales Incentive, a separate rule unrelated to whether it counts as a meeting.
Lead
Any Bookings CSV row with a recognized SDR Owner and a non-blank Creation Date. Rows that don't match either are dropped before anything else on the dashboard is computed.
Lead → Closed Rate
Closed deals ÷ all leads from the same creation-date cohort. Recent periods always read low — a lead created this week hasn't had time to close yet — this is a running tally that rises as those leads close later, not a same-period conversion rate.
Lead Rules (Strict/Loose toggle)
Top controls bar, defaults to Strict. Governs the Summary Cards ONLY (Lead Volume, Meetings Booked, Meeting Booked Rate, Leads Qualified, % Leads Qualified, Non-Responsive Rate) — every other section (charts, Mandate Scoreboard, Sales Funnel, AE Baseline, Capacity & Cost) is unaffected; the Commission Calculator always uses Strict-equivalent rules regardless of this toggle. Strict: Duplicate-record and Wrong-info leads are dropped from every count (including Lead Volume itself), and Meetings Booked requires a genuine Meeting Status (isMeetingBookedStrict — an Instant Close never counts) — matches a hand-counted golden-truth export on every Leads: filter (B2B only, B2C only, All leads, Commission View), typically within a couple of leads. Loose: the older, broader counts (duplicates included, Instant Closes credited as meetings) — useful for comparing against how the dashboard used to read.
Meeting → Closed Rate
Closed deals ÷ meetings done from the same creation-date cohort. Same "reads low until leads catch up" caveat as the other cohort rates. Only exists in the Sales Funnel — the Mandate Scoreboard doesn't have this row. AE Baseline column shows the playbook's static ~50% benchmark ("Online meetings convert at approximately 50%") instead of a computed number — 66% of the AE-era's closed deals have a blank Meeting Status, so a computed ratio here comes out to either ~93-96% or 266%+ depending on how those blanks are treated, neither of which is trustworthy.
Meeting Booked
Two versions in use. General (charts, Mandate Scoreboard, Sales Funnel, AE Baseline, Capacity & Cost's own SDR/AE tables): Meeting Status is Scheduled, Done, Rejected, or Not Atended, or the deal was an Instant Close (unless the Auto-Booked Meetings Toggle is set to Excluded). Strict/commission (Summary Cards under Lead Rules: Strict, the Commission Calculator, the Meeting Quality Multiplier, the printable report): Meeting Status only — an Instant Close never counts, regardless of the Auto-Booked toggle, matching a hand-counted golden-truth export. Either way, "Meeting Requested" doesn't count on its own — roughly three-quarters of those end up disqualified, so it means "asked for," not "confirmed."
Meeting Held (Meetings Done)
Meeting Status is Meeting Done, or an instant close (unless the Auto-Booked Meetings Toggle is set to Excluded). Narrower than Meeting Booked, which also counts still-upcoming Scheduled meetings.
Meeting Not Attended (No-Show)
Meeting Status = Meeting Not Atended or Meeting Rejected, as a share of that period's booked meetings (Scheduled + Done + Not Atended + Rejected, minus instant closes). Same combined numerator the commission Quality Multiplier uses — a rejected meeting is still a booked meeting that didn't happen as scheduled, same as a no-show.
Meeting Rejected
The AE declining a booked meeting — a qualification call, not a client no-show. Counts toward Meeting Booked and the no-show rate's denominator, but never its numerator; tracked separately as AE Disqualified / Handoff Quality.
MTD / WTD
"Month/week to date" — marks whichever column is the still-in-progress current calendar month or week. Its raw counts are naturally lower than a full period's, simply because the period isn't over yet.
Non-Responsive Rate
Two versions in use. Broad (Summary Card and its chart): Disqualified reason is "Not responsive," or Sales status is "Non respondant" — includes leads still mid-cadence, not yet given up on. Terminal (Mandate Scoreboard only, to match the strategic briefing's 29% baseline): Disqualified reason = "Not responsive" alone — leads permanently given up on as unreachable, excluding the transient mid-cadence status.
On-Hours / Off-Hours
Whether a lead arrived during an SDR's own working shift (their solo shift plus the team's overlap window), computed in that SDR's own personal timezone — EST for MJ and Gospel, CET for Mark and Levi — not Dubai time. Weekends are always off-hours for everyone, regardless of clock time.
Pay ÷ Revenue
Sales Funnel row (Month over Month view, individual SDR tables only — behind the "Show pay ÷ revenue" toggle in the Per-SDR Breakdown, off by default so it's never accidentally visible while screen-sharing). That SDR's total pay for the calendar month ÷ USD-equivalent closed revenue from leads created that month — same creation-date cohort and Instant Close exclusion as commission's own revenue figure. Pay is entered by hand (click a value to set/edit it); lower is better, so the delta color is inverted from every other funnel row. No AE Baseline column — pay data doesn't exist for the pre-SDR era.
QAL Funnel by SDR (B2B/B2C)
Capacity & Cost section, same chosen month as the rest of that section. Uses the standard Qualified Lead definition below for QAL, and the standard Meeting Booked / Meeting Not Attended definitions for Meeting and Meeting Rejected. The one thing this table does that nowhere else on the dashboard does: Duplicate-record and Wrong-info leads are dropped from the B2B/B2C pool entirely before anything is counted, so the B2B Leads denominator itself already has them removed too — everywhere else, those leads still count toward Lead Volume, just never toward Qualified. QAL %, Meeting %, and Net Meeting % (Meeting − Rejected) are each that count ÷ B2B Leads. Small (single-digit) drift against a hand-counted golden-truth export is expected — Creation Date is Romania-local and gets converted to Dubai time, so a handful of leads right at a month boundary can land in a different bucket than a manual read of the raw export.
Qualified → Closed Rate
Closed deals ÷ qualified leads from the same creation-date cohort. Same "reads low until leads catch up" caveat as the other cohort rates.
Qualified Lead
Reset 2026-07-23 against a hand-counted "golden truth" export until every cell matched exactly. Sales status must be Hot, Warm, Closed, Lost, Qualified SDR, or any refund status (Full/Deposit/Partial refund) — nothing else counts, including Pending, Quote Sent, and "Disqualified AE" (an AE bouncing a lead back no longer earns it special treatment). A Duplicate-record or Wrong-info lead never counts, whatever its Sales status says — though it still counts toward Lead Volume everywhere except the QAL Funnel by SDR table, which drops it entirely (see that entry). If Sales status is blank, an explicit SQL status = Qualified still counts — a legacy fallback that mostly applies to AE Baseline data. This replaces the previous broader rule (status allowlist OR any meeting ever booked, regardless of later status changes) dashboard-wide — every chart, card, Mandate Scoreboard row, Sales Funnel, AE Baseline, Capacity & Cost table, and Commission figure now uses this same stricter definition.
SDR Capacity
Capacity & Cost section, last calendar month. Leads (B2B), Qualified, Meetings Booked, and Closed for that SDR, plus an editable Capacity target — defaults to 275/month, the onboarding brief's own steady-state target per Coordinator once the team reaches 6 people. Leads shown in red when they exceed the target.
SDR Disqualified
Sales status is exactly "Disqualified" — the SDR's own screening call. Distinct from AE Disqualified, which is the AE overruling a qualification after handoff.
SDR Pay (Capacity & Cost)
Prefers the actual reported monthly pay (the same figures entered in the Sales Funnel's Pay ÷ Revenue row) for that exact month when one exists; falls back to the Commission Calculator's computed Total Compensation (Base + Bonus + all three incentives, quality-multiplier applied — same figure as that SDR's Commission card) only when nothing's been reported yet, marked with * when this happens. Feeds Total Compensation, Cost per Qualified Lead, and Pay ÷ Revenue (that SDR's cost ÷ their closed revenue — a ratio, so it stays visible even with $ figures hidden).
Speed-to-Lead
Median (not mean) minutes from a lead's creation to that SDR's first outbound call, matched by phone number against the Aircall call log. A lead created before the call log's own earliest call is excluded as ineligible to measure; a match found more than 48 hours later is treated as unreliable and discarded rather than counted as a real (very slow) response.
Work Week (Saturday–Friday)
The 7-day week every "Weekly" view on this dashboard uses, running Saturday through Friday rather than the Monday-start Western convention — matches the team's actual working week.
